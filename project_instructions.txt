COM SCI 188: Introduction to Robotics (winter 2026) Final Project
Option 2: Open Project - Your idea!
We encourage you to pursue an open project if you’re interested in building something
creative, ambitious, or personally meaningful. This is your chance to take everything
you’ve learned and apply it to an idea you’re passionate about.
Your project must include a robotics component, but beyond that, you’re free to
explore any tools, platforms, or topics that inspire you. Whether it’s artistic, interactive,
research-driven, or just something fun—go for it!
Just make sure to get your idea approved by the instructor or a TA.
Example topics:
● AI Agent for Robotic Control: Implement an AI agent that converts high-level
natural language instructions into executable robot actions in robosuite. The
system must: (1) parse at least 3 distinct task instructions (e.g., “stack the
blocks,” “sort by size”), (2) generate a sequence of low-level actions or
subgoals, and (3) successfully execute the task in simulation. Include a clear
mapping from language → plan → control.
● Shared Control (Human + Autonomy): Design a shared-control system in
robosuite where the user controls at least one degree of freedom (e.g.,
end-effector position via joystick), while an autonomous policy controls another
component (e.g., grip force, vertical stabilization, or obstacle avoidance).
Demonstrate improved task performance or usability compared to manual-only
control.
● Learning to Act (Reinforcement Learning): Train a robosuite robot using a
standard RL algorithm (e.g., PPO, SAC) to complete a defined manipulation task
(e.g., door opening, nut assembly, pick-and-place). The project must include: (1)
task definition, (2) reward design, (3) training curve visualization, and (4)
quantitative evaluation of success rate.
● Multimodal Control (Gesture or Voice): Implement a gesture-based or
speech-based interface that triggers at least 3 distinct robot actions in robosuite
(e.g., “pick up,” “stop,” “reset”). The system must include input processing

● Expressive Motion (Emotional Trajectories): Design and implement at least 3
distinct expressive motion styles for a robot arm (e.g., excited, sad, neutral) by
